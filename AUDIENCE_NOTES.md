# The Gilded Eye — Audience Notes

## Target: Founders & entrepreneurs in decision paralysis

### Pain points (initial research — 2026-03-19)

Observed themes from current articles/discussions about decision fatigue, analysis paralysis, and bad decisions under pressure:

- **Analysis paralysis / too many options**
  - People stall on decisions not because they lack intelligence, but because they are flooded with options and competing frameworks.
  - Over-collecting advice, systems, and frameworks leads to “paralysis” — the decision feels heavier the more input they gather.

- **Decision fatigue and repeated re-discussion**
  - Leaders re-open decisions that were supposedly settled last week.
  - Teams cycle through the same debates multiple times because no decision was made with a clear frame.
  - Burnout often shows up as friction, hesitation, and rehashing rather than a single obvious breaking point.

- **Urgency-driven decisions**
  - Decisions get compressed into frantic execution when deadlines loom.
  - Urgency is treated as a reason instead of a symptom.

- **Advice-seeking as avoidance**
  - Founders describe “chasing tactics” and hunting for the “right answer” like it’s hiding in someone else’s thread, course, or framework.
  - Asking more people for input becomes a way to delay owning the decision.

### Where they hang out (initial scan)

- X / Twitter:
  - Indie hackers, bootstrappers, and ADHD/entrepreneurship accounts discussing:
    - Overthinking launches.
    - Too many conflicting business models.
    - Fear of committing to one strategy.
- Substack / newsletters:
  - Long-form essays on decision fatigue and the cognitive load of running a business.
- Leadership / management blogs:
  - Articles on decision fatigue at work, repeated re-decision, and the cost of unclear framing.

### Language patterns worth mirroring (without becoming motivational)

- "I keep circling the same decision and never feel done."
- "Every time I ask for another opinion, the decision gets heavier."
- "I’m smart enough to know what’s at stake, but not clear enough to move."
- "We keep re-litigating decisions that should already be locked."
- "By the time the deadline hits, it’s panic, not clarity."

These notes should evolve over time as new patterns show up in founder/entrepreneur conversations.
